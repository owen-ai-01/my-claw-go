## 2. What Data We Process

### 2.1 Account and Communication Data

- **Basic Account Data** (if you create an account):
  Name (if provided), email address, language/locale, registration date
- **Access Data**: Login times, account status
- **Communication**: Content of your inquiries to us (e.g., via email or form) and our responses

### 2.2 Image and Content Data

- **URL Input**: The URL you provide is processed to extract content.
- **Generated Content**: Scripts, storyboards, images, and videos generated from your URL input.
- **Processed Content**: 
    - **Firecrawl**: Used to crawl and extract content from the provided URL.
    - **LLM**: Large Language Models are used to generate storyboards and scripts from the extracted content.
    - **Replicate**: Used to generate AI images and assets based on the storyboard.
    - **Shotstack**: Used to compile and render the final video.
- **Metadata**: File type, file size, upload time, technical processing information.

We process this data exclusively to provide you with the requested video features, display results, and enable downloads.

### 2.3 Payment and Billing Data

If you use paid features, payment data (e.g., card data) is processed by our payment service provider (e.g., Stripe).
We only receive information necessary for invoicing, allocation, and support (e.g., name, email, billing address, last digits of the card, transaction ID).

### 2.4 Technical Data and Usage Data

- **Log Data**: IP address, date and time of access, requested URLs, HTTP status codes, transferred data volume, referrer URL, user agent (browser & OS)
- **Device and Session Data**: Device type, screen resolution, language settings, approximate region/location (derived from IP), session duration
- **Usage Behavior**: Which pages and features you use, clicks, scroll behavior, error messages

This data typically arises during the technical operation of the website and is stored in log files.

### 2.5 Other Data Provided by You

In addition to the categories mentioned above, we may process other personal data that you voluntarily provide to us in connection with the use of our services.
This may include in particular:

- Content of feedback forms, support requests, or reviews
- Information about your preferences and settings in the account or your profile
- Other information you transmit during communication with us

We process this data only insofar as it is necessary to answer your inquiries, improve our services, or fulfill legal obligations.

## 3. Cookies and Similar Technologies

### 3.1 Only Necessary Cookies

We use exclusively **necessary cookies** (Essential Cookies) that are strictly required for the basic functionality of our website. These cookies cannot be disabled as they are necessary for the proper operation of the website.

We do not use analytics, tracking, or marketing cookies. We do not use cookies for usage analysis, advertising, or profiling.

**Necessary Cookies Used:**

- **Session Cookies**: To maintain your login session and ensure functionality during your visit
- **Security Cookies**: To protect against fraud and abuse
- **Language Cookies**: To save your language setting (e.g., NEXT_LOCALE)
- **Theme Cookies**: To save your theme setting (light/dark)

You can partially or fully disable cookies in your browser settings. In this case, however, some functions of the website may be restricted or unusable.

## 4. How We Process Your URL and Content

### 4.1 Processing Steps

1.  **URL Submission**: You submit a URL to our service.
2.  **Content Extraction**: We use **Firecrawl** to extract text and relevant information from the URL.
3.  **Script & Storyboard**: An **LLM** analyzes the extracted content to create a video script and storyboard.
4.  **Asset Generation**: **Replicate** generates visual assets (images) based on the storyboard.
5.  **Video Compilation**: **Shotstack** renders the final video using the generated assets and script.
6.  **Result Delivery**: The final video is stored and made available for you to download.

### 4.2 Storage Duration

- **Input Data**: URLs and extracted content are processed temporarily.
- **Generated Assets**: Intermediate assets (images, scripts) and final videos are kept for a limited period to allow for download and history viewing, after which they are regularly deleted.
- **Temporary Files at Service Providers**: Data transmitted to third-party providers (Firecrawl, Replicate, Shotstack) is handled according to their respective data retention policies.

**Storage of Images and Regular Deletion:**

Uploaded images are only needed for processing and are deleted thereafter.
We delete images regularly at scheduled intervals so that they are not stored longer than necessary.
The exact deletion period may vary, but data is removed promptly.

## 5. Legal Basis under GDPR

We process your personal data based on the following legal bases:

- **Art. 6 (1) lit. b GDPR – Contract Performance**
  To provide the services requested by you (e.g., account, image processing, download, support).

- **Art. 6 (1) lit. f GDPR – Legitimate Interest**
  To improve and develop our services, for error analysis, to prevent misuse, and for IT security (including log files for technical purposes).

- **Art. 6 (1) lit. a GDPR – Consent**
  For certain optional processing, e.g., marketing emails, provided we explicitly ask you for this.

## 9. Your Rights under GDPR

Regarding your personal data, you have in particular the following rights:

- **Access** (Art. 15 GDPR): You can request information about which personal data we process about you.
- **Rectification** (Art. 16 GDPR): You can request the correction of incorrect or completion of incomplete data.
- **Erasure** (Art. 17 GDPR): You can request the deletion of your data ("Right to be Forgotten"), provided no legal or contractual retention obligations oppose this.
- **Restriction of Processing** (Art. 18 GDPR): You can request that we process your data only to a limited extent.
- **Data Portability** (Art. 20 GDPR): You can request that we provide you with certain data in a structured, common, and machine-readable format.
- **Objection** (Art. 21 GDPR): You can object to the processing of your data if it is based on Art. 6 (1) lit. f GDPR (legitimate interest).
- **Withdrawal of Consent** (Art. 7 (3) GDPR): You can withdraw consent given to us at any time with effect for the future.

To exercise your rights, you can contact us via email at any time: **support@myclawgo.com**.
You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a data protection supervisory authority, in particular in the member state of your residence or workplace.
